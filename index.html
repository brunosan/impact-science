The "Impact Science" Project

Facts don't change the world. People do. Specially in complex situations, facts and Science is only part of the mix. There could be other reasons,like implementation, economic, implementation reasons, political reasons, strategic reasons, … Science is an important one, but is only part of the equation; and definitely is not only about pushing the facts by publishing the facts. Science should be fully engaged as a tool, not a body of knowledge.

How do we get there? what skills are needed? Who are the success stories? What's the risk? What is the role of Data, Facts and Science in today's Society, and its driving forces, such as the 4.0 Industrial Revolution, migrations, populism, climate change or social media.

The trouble with Science Today: Publish and perish

Publish or Perish

As a scientist your credentials, your funding, your job, depends on publications and the citations of those publications. So if the expectation is to understand everything, you only have time to do what ends up in a paper.

Reproducilibty Crisis

According to a 2016 poll of 1,500 scientists, 70% of them failed to reproduce another scientist's experiments (50% failed to reproduce their own experiment).

Segmenting Knowledge

As science evolves we have gone deeper and deeper into fields that needed more specialization. We started to constrict science into silos, and silos within silos: silos of peers, silos of tools, silos of knowledge.

Elite Academia, or else

When we train scientists, not only the best ones go to Academia, but also no getting an academic post is a sign of not being "good enough".

Science 4.0: Beyond Academia.

Shifting from Science as body of Knowledge towards a practical toolset for the World.

Data Science

Data Science exploded because the private sector needed to make decisions based on their data, more than they needed research. Roughly 50% of Data Scientists are former academic researchers.

Common Tools

Virtually all STEM education shares various degrees of the same logical, mathematical and abstracted logic tools. From linear regressions, to waves, statistics and the scientific method of falsable hypothesis via experimentation.

It's not about the facts

If it were about facts, e.g. Climate Change would not be a debate. There's much more in how people and societies act of facts. There's implementation, politics, economy, strategy, common sense, culture ... Facts and Science are only part of it.

Digital Dividends

The digital world, enabled by Science and Tecnology provides compounding benefits. Potentially Everywhere; but in practice economically, demographically and geographically unevenly distributed, which yields growing development gaps.
